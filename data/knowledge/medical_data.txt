# Sample Medical Knowledge for RAG

Test: Hemoglobin (Hb)
Reference Range: 
- Men: 13.5 to 17.5 g/dL
- Women: 12.0 to 15.5 g/dL
Explanation: Hemoglobin is a protein in red blood cells that carries oxygen. Low levels may indicate anemia.

Test: White Blood Cell (WBC) Count
Reference Range: 4,500 to 11,000 cells per microliter (mcL)
Explanation: WBCs help the body fight infection. High counts suggest infection or inflammation.

Test: Platelet Count
Reference Range: 150,000 to 450,000 platelets/mcL
Explanation: Platelets help with blood clotting. Low levels (thrombocytopenia) can cause bleeding issues.

Test: HbA1c (Glycated Hemoglobin)
Reference Range:
- Normal: Below 5.7%
- Prediabetes: 5.7% to 6.4%
- Diabetes: 6.5% or higher
Explanation: HbA1c measures average blood sugar over the last 3 months. Used for diabetes management.

Test: Fasting Blood Sugar
Reference Range:
- Normal: 70–99 mg/dL
- Prediabetes: 100–125 mg/dL
- Diabetes: 126 mg/dL or higher
Explanation: Sugar levels in blood after at least 8 hours of fasting. Used to screen for diabetes.
